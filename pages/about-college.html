About Our College

New Arts, Commerce and Science College, Parner

Parner: A Historic Town

Parner has a great historical, cultural, social and spiritual background. It is connected to well-known Supa industrial belt having industries of almost varied fields. Parner was named after Rishi Parashar, whose son Maharishi Ved Vyas wrote famous epic "Mahabharata".

It is the birthplace of Senapati Bapat, from where he continued Indian independence movement and Mulshi Satyagraha. Anna Hazare, an Indian social activist who led movements to promote rural development, anti-corruption and Jan Lokpal Bill is native of Ralegan Siddhi, 10 km away from Parner.

Establishment & Growth

New Arts, Commerce and Science College, Parner is one of the active educational institute established in the rural area of Ahmednagar district in the form of Arts and Commerce faculties since 1977 and extended in 1992 in the form of Science faculty.

It is affiliated to Savitribai Phule Pune University, Pune and functioning by the principle of participatory management in order to reach to the weaker sections of society. The college is recognized by UGC under Section 2(f) and 12(B), UGC Act 1956.
